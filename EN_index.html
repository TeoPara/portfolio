Preliminary interest // â€” games that introduced me to the concept of programming

The first game I've ever played must've been Little Big Planet... on the PS3, when I was around 4 years old.
I'm sure that if that game weren't a SANDBOX game, that I would be less likely to be where I am today.
That platformer game is a bastion of creativity, it allows you to create a lot of cool stuff! It has a "circuit" system- You can add various components like logical gates or math operators or sensors onto things, and then wire things together to create mechanics in your levels. This is the most important part of the sandbox experience. And you could technically call it programming, in the broader definition of the word. It's like visual scripting.

The next important game that I came across is called Manyland. It's a 2D tile-based sandbox roleplay browser game where everything consists of pixel-art drawn by the players. You can create a world and fill it with either other people's things or stuff you've created yourself. But the important part is that the game also has it's own programming language that allows you to create interactive objects, game mechanics, systems. So you can create a world where you've got a ping-pong game, or a working chess board, etc., and have other players play it with you.
I have spent a LOT of time in this game, just coding stuff with its custom language. But I've also spent a good amount of time doing pixel-art! //(which quite paid off down the line)

My first real programming experience

..But this changed when I found an open-source game called Barotrauma in 2019. I learned C# by looking through that codebase and changing random things around until I figured out what they did and how they worked. Normally the game is co-op where the players play as humans on a submarine. They go and explore and they'll occasionally encounter some AI monsters in the water on the outside outside and fight them. What I've done instead is that the submarine is frozen in place so there's no exploration, and 50% of the players get randomly chosen to play as a monster instead. So it's an entirely PvP focused mode with players as humans vs players as monsters.
